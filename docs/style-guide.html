IV
Layout

Measure governs the reading column.

Line length is locked by character count, not pixels, so it stays correct regardless of font loading or user zoom. Three widths for three roles.

measure · 65ch · body prose

The College Football Playoff, as presently structured, treats the regular season as an audition for an audition. A 24-team Champions Conference would replace the audition with the thing itself — and make every September game a referendum on belonging. This paragraph is set at the default body measure of 65 characters.

measure-wide · 78ch · lead paragraphs

A lead paragraph sits just below a display headline, set in italic and at 22px, and is allowed a slightly wider measure because the larger type gives fewer characters per line anyway.

measure-narrow · 48ch · captions & figure text

Fig. 4 — A narrow measure makes captions feel deliberate rather than overflowing. Used for figure descriptions, sidenotes, and any text meant to sit in the margin.

§ II
The thesis

Every September game, a referendum.

The College Football Playoff, as presently structured, treats the regular season as an audition for an audition. There is a better arrangement.

A 24-team Champions Conference, organized on merit and refreshed annually by promotion and relegation, would replace the audition with the thing itself. The top division plays for the national title. The second division plays to join them. Every game in every week carries stakes that reach past the scoreboard.

Restraint is status. When in doubt, subtract.

The objections are predictable. Tradition, schedule, television contracts — all real, all answerable. The harder question is whether the sport, which has always organized itself in the image of the money that pays for it, can organize itself around something else.

VIII
Usage notes

Rules the system assumes.

  • One display per page. The display size (58px) is used exactly once — the page's main title. After that, H1 takes over.
  • Oxblood is rare. Three to five appearances per page, maximum. If the color shows up more than that, something else needs to give.
  • Old-style figures in prose, tabular in data. Body text uses old-style (varying-height) numerals automatically. Anything in a table or standings block gets .tabular for aligned lining figures.
  • Never exceed 65ch for prose. Use .prose or max-width: var(--measure). Wider paragraphs fatigue the eye within a few hundred words.
  • Eyebrow labels pair with H2 sections. Not H1. Not H3. An eyebrow above a display or H1 is visual clutter; above an H3 it implies a structural level that isn't there.
  • Section numerals are used on /proposal and /spec. Not on /, not on /about. They signal a document with internal structure, not a page.
  • Mobile drops the top of the type scale. Display, H1, H2, and H3 shrink below 640px. Body and smaller sizes stay constant — we don't reduce reading size on small screens.
